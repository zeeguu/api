A small ask for your polyglot expertise

Hello {first_name},

You might remember Zeeguu — the app I presented at last year's Polyglot Gathering. You signed up for an account back then, which is how you ended up on this short list. I'd love your help with a study — one that might help you too.

Zeeguu has changed a lot since then: there's a mobile app now (iOS + Android), audio lessons on any topic you choose, and LLM-based simplification that adapts any article to your level in one of your target languages.

It's still meant as a humble extra tool alongside your other ways of learning. If you were going to read an article anyway, why not read it in your target language at your current level? If you have a 10-minute bike ride or walk, why not spend it on an audio lesson?

The ask is light:

1. Try Zeeguu for a few days in any of your languages — a good chance to chisel the less advanced ones before the Gathering.
2. Fill in this short form to confirm you're up for a 25-minute conversation, at the Gathering or by video call afterwards: https://forms.gle/6KCEwihWnkdWKVuG6

Findings will feed Zeeguu's roadmap and possibly a talk/paper on how polyglots use comprehensible input tools across multiple languages.
