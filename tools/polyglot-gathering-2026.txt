Polyglot Gathering 2026: a small ask from Zeeguu

{hello} {first_name},

I'm Mircea, one of the researchers behind Zeeguu — the open-source platform that helps you learn languages through authentic content. I'm writing because the next Polyglot Gathering is coming up (27 May) and I'd love your help with something.

You created a Zeeguu account one year ago, during the Gathering — some of you I met in person, some came to the presentation, some I haven't met yet. Either way, that's how you ended up on this short list.

A lot has changed since then: there's now a mobile app (iOS + Android), audio lessons on any topic you choose, and LLM-based simplification that takes any article in any language and adapts it to *your* level in one of your target languages.

Even with all these additions, Zeeguu still aims to be a humble top-up rather than a replacement for your other ways of learning. If you were going to read an article anyway, why not read it in your target language? If you have a 10-minute bike ride, why not spend it on an audio lesson about something you actually care about?

The ask is light:

1. Try Zeeguu for a few days in any of your languages — a good chance to chisel the less advanced ones before the Gathering.
2. Confirm you're up for a 25-minute conversation — at the Gathering or by video call afterwards — via this short form: https://forms.gle/6KCEwihWnkdWKVuG6

Findings will feed Zeeguu's roadmap and possibly a talk/paper on how polyglots use comprehensible input tools across multiple languages.
